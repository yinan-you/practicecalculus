## Part (a)

Apply the product rule to $f(x) = x^2 e^x$.

$$
f'(x) = 2x e^x + x^2 e^x = e^x(x^2 + 2x)
$$

## Part (b)

By the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus,

$$
\int_0^1 f'(x)\,dx = f(1) - f(0) = e - 0 = e
$$

## Answer

(a) $f'(x) = e^x(x^2 + 2x)$

(b) $e$
